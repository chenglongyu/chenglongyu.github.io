Syllabus

Representation theory is a central topic in modern mathematics. This course will begin with an introduction to symmetries, Platonic solids, and the basic concepts of group theory. We will then explore linear representations of finite groups and character theory. The final part of the course will focus on the representations of symmetries arising from Platonic solids, leading to the McKay correspondence and root systems.
